GOOGLE STADIA

July 2019

Stadia, simply put is a cloud gaming service capable of streaming video games in 4K resolution at 60 frames per second

Google started working on the project ‘Stream’ in 2016, inviting beta testers in October 2018 with access to Assassin’s Creed Odyssey. Stadia got formally introduced during the Game Developers Conference in March 2019.

History

Google developed its own HID-class USB controller which is said to include google assistant. This could connect via Wi-Fi directly to Google datacenter where the game is running, to reduce input latency.

Google’s Console

Google plans to invest in various studios to develop games for Stadia. Google also plans to build its own studios and creating its own games.

Development of Games for Stadia

Google will be spending $13 billion in infrastructure and capital expenditure in 2019. They will be launching this year in U.S., Canada, the U.K. and most of Europe. They were live in 12 data centers in the U.S. with the project Stream for the beta testers.

Stadia in 2019

Stadia is anticipated to reach 120 frames per second at 8K resolution. A huge roaster of games to be available on Stadia are said to be released in August. They have brought Jade Raymond (Vice-president at EA Motive, 2018) to imagine the future of Stadia.

Future of Stadia

1)Lack of connectivity and internet speeds all over the blood.
2) The internet usage could be dozens of gigabytes requiring speeds upto 30 megabits per second for 4K/60fps experience.
3) The lowest resolution Google is willing to go is 720px.
4) The vision of Google has always been Universal Accessibility but this product defies this idea.
5) This has the potential to create a huge power surge for Google.
